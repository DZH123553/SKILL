# Bill Gurley's Investment Portfolio: A Research Report

This report provides a detailed analysis of the investment portfolio and track record of Bill Gurley, a general partner at the venture capital firm Benchmark. The research focuses on his key investments in companies that have significantly impacted the technology landscape.

## Detailed Investment Analysis

### Uber

Bill Gurley led Benchmark's investment in Uber in 2011 [1]. His investment thesis was centered on the belief that Uber was not merely a digital taxi service but a platform with the potential for global expansion and the introduction of adjacent services. He recognized the company's optionality to evolve and capture a larger share of the transportation market.

> Gurley's investment in Uber is considered one of his most successful. He has publicly discussed his belief in the company's long-term potential and its ability to transform transportation [1].

### OpenTable

Bill Gurley was an early investor in OpenTable, the online restaurant reservation company. His involvement with the company culminated in a successful initial public offering (IPO) under the ticker OPEN. The company was later acquired by The Priceline Group, delivering a significant return for early investors [2].

### Zillow

Bill Gurley has been a long-time board member of Zillow, the online real estate marketplace. His guidance and expertise have been instrumental in the company's growth and strategic direction. He was reappointed to the board in 2024, underscoring his continued commitment to the company's success [2].

### GrubHub

Benchmark, led by Bill Gurley, invested in GrubHub's Series C funding round on November 15, 2010. Gurley was impressed by the company's rapid growth and its ability to scale its operations and build a strong brand.

> "Since its initial launch in 2004, GrubHub's growth has been extraordinary. I am excited to work with two remarkable founders in Matt Maloney and Mike Evans, and I am also thrilled about the opportunity to partner again with OpenTable founder and GrubHub board Chairman Chuck Templeton. The team has clearly demonstrated their ability to successfully launch new markets, scale their business, and build a brand that is highly valued by its customers." - Bill Gurley [3]

GrubHub's success continued, leading to an IPO under the ticker GRUB.

### Stitch Fix

On October 18, 2013, Benchmark led a $12 million Series B investment in Stitch Fix, an online personal styling service. As part of the investment, Bill Gurley joined the company's board of directors. Gurley was particularly attracted to the company's organic growth, which was largely driven by word-of-mouth recommendations from satisfied customers. He also recognized the power of Stitch Fix's data-driven approach to personal styling, which set it apart from traditional retailers [4].
