# Supplemental Research: insights4vc Deep Dive on Ribbit Capital

Source: https://insights4vc.substack.com/p/what-ribbit-sees-before-everyone

## Key Data Points

### Origin Story
- Grew up in 1980s Venezuela with bank runs, currency devaluations, 100%+ inflation
- Age 6: wrote letter to tooth fairy asking for US dollars instead of bolivars
- Age 17: co-founded broker-dealer (Heptagon Group) exploiting local banking quirk for double interest on weekends during high inflation
- Early 20s: partnered with Wences Casares to build Patagon (one of LatAm's first online banks)
- Santander acquired Patagon in March 2000 for ~$750M (just before dot-com crash)
- Banco Lemon (Brazil, 2003): targeting underbanked, became Brazil's largest private microfinance institution, sold to Banco do Brasil in 2009
- Stints as interim CEO of OpenBank (Spanish online bank) and co-CEO of Bling Nation (Palo Alto mobile payments)
- Built and sold 5 financial services companies across 3 continents before writing a VC check

### Ribbit Capital Fund Details
- Fund I (2012): $100M - deliberately small to "keep him in check"
  - Theme: "last-mile fintech" - consumer-facing financial apps
  - ~12 companies, globally diverse
  - Direct Bitcoin position as strategic exposure
  - Early investor in Coinbase
  - LPs: high-net-worth allies, BBVA, SVB
  
- Fund II (2014, ~$125M):
  - Blue-chip LPs: Emory, MIT, Duke endowments; Page family; Sequoia Heritage, Iconiq
  - Theme: double down on emerging winners, expand adjacencies
  - Backed Robinhood in 2014
  - Malka joined Robinhood's board

- Fund III (2017):
  - Expanded into global neo-banks and crypto infrastructure

### Key Investment Cases
- Coinbase: Fund I investment (~4% stake), went public April 2021 at $85B, stake worth ~$3.3B, ~33x of $100M Fund I
- Robinhood: Backed in 2014, Malka on board; during 2021 crisis, Ribbit participated in $3B financing deal
- Nubank: Ribbit invested early, became board member; IPO 2021 at $40B+
- Credit Karma: Early investment, acquired by Intuit for $7.1B in 2020

### 2025 Token Factory Thesis
- Tokens as machine-legible claims on identity, assets, and expertise
- Agents as the new economic interface
- Capital formation at internet speed
- "Ribbit is neither a fintech momentum fund nor a crypto tourist, but a thesis-driven platform built to operate through regime change"

### Ribbit's Edge (Differentiation)
1. Regulatory fluency
2. Systems thinking
3. Conviction underwriting in compliance-heavy categories
4. Global focus from day one (US, Canada, UK, Brazil, Spain, South Africa)
5. Operator-led (not just financial investors)
6. Extreme availability to founders
