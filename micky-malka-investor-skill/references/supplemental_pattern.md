# Supplemental Research: Micky Malka Investment Pattern (FutureHumanism)

## The Malka Pattern (explicitly identified)
Step 1: Identify a structural shift in how money works
Step 2: Find the team building infrastructure for that shift
Step 3: Get in early and stay involved
Step 4: Wait for the world to catch up

## Key Data Points
- AUM: $14.5 billion across multiple funds
- Track Record: 160+ portfolio companies, 35+ unicorns
- Forbes Midas List: 5x
- Former Bitcoin Foundation board member

## Coinbase (2013)
- Co-led Series A when Bitcoin was under $100
- Ribbit's first fund had invested directly in Bitcoin earlier that year (not in a company, in Bitcoin itself)
- Experience revealed pain points: custody was terrifying, converting to fiat was miserable, security was a nightmare
- Coinbase solved all three
- Described internally as "financial infrastructure" not "crypto startup"
- ~4% stake worth $3.3B at 2021 direct listing
- 33x Return on Fund I

## Robinhood (2014)
- Backed $13M Series A
- Every major brokerage charged $7-$10 per trade
- "Most investors saw a feature. Malka saw a generational shift."
- Not competing with Schwab on price, competing with Instagram on experience
- Malka joined the board
- By IPO: $46B valuation on opening day
- Vlad Tenev calls Malka his "mentor"
- Ribbit has profited on its stake by more than $5 billion (Forbes)

## Nubank (2016)
- Brazilian banks charging monthly fees just for holding accounts
- 5 banks controlled 80%+ of deposits
- Same pattern: overpriced, poor experience, ripe for disruption
- Grew to 100M+ customers across Brazil, Mexico, Colombia
- IPO valuation: $41B
